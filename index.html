Wrap yourself in petals for armor - Hayley Williams

Released in three parts over the course of this spring, Petals for Armor can be viewed as a trilogy of five songs each, where Williams explores her changing coping mechanisms in the midst of hardship

Read More

This solo debut from the frontwoman of pop-punk stadium stars Paramore is a riot of lust, funk and femininity

Williams explained the inspiration behind the title of the album is due to her belief that "the best way for me to protect myself is to be vulnerable."
The underlying message of Hayley Williams’ stunning solo album is this: wear your flaws like a coat of gleaming armour and find strength in being open and vulnerable.

A REVIEW OF HAYLEY WILLIAMS’ ‘PETALS FOR ARMOR’

Petals for Armor was not what I expected at all from Hayley Williams, but I’m not sure what I did expect. I had a feeling she would veer away from the Paramore sound, but who knew in what direction? I did expect a beautiful mix of dark and beauty though, and Petals for Armor delivers on that front. The winding, ethereal sounds of “Roses/Lotus/Violet/Iris” and the vampiric scratch of “Creepin’” exist and succeed on the same piece of work, which in itself is a major achievement.

- Baylee from atwood magazine
